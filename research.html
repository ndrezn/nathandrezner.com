I'm involved in several research projects at McGill, all of which are ongoing.



Wikipedia

Since Fall 2018, I've been involved in a research project studying the salient patterns of language and behavior on Wikipedia. This project has evolved over time from a language-driven study of argument on Wikipedia to a network analysis-driven study of user behavior on Wikipedia.

The project is overseen by Profs. Andrew Piper and Richard Jean So as part of the .txtLab at McGill, and in Summer 2018 I was awarded an ARIA for my work on the project.

More information on the project, as well as some preliminary findings, is available on GitHub.



Computational Linguistics

Through the McGill Computational & Quantitative Linguistics Lab, I'm part of a research project studying productivity in language. I am working on reconstructing a qualitative model of productivity in an algorithmic representation to test its validity based on real-world productive patterns. More generally, the MCQLL studies fundamental questions about language acquisition, processing, use in society, and change over time.
